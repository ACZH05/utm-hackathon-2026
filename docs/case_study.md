**Case Study 1:
Precision Urban Agriculture for Vertical Farming
(IoT System/ Web Dashboard/ Mobile Application)**

**1. Introduction**
Rapid urbanization, shrinking arable land, and climate change are putting immense
pressure on traditional food supply chains. In densely populated areas, food security and
the carbon footprint of transporting produce are growing concerns. Vertical farming offers
a space-efficient, sustainable alternative by growing crops in stacked layers within
controlled indoor environments, making it possible to produce food directly within city
limits

**2. Problem Statement**
Managing a vertical farm manually is highly resource-intensive and prone to human
error. Inconsistent lighting, improper nutrient delivery, and fluctuating climate control can
quickly lead to suboptimal crop yields, crop disease, wasted water, and excessively high
energy consumption. Currently, many urban farmers and hobbyists lack access to
affordable, data-driven automation tools needed to precisely monitor and optimize plant
growth around the clock.

**3. Challenge**
Develop an intelligent, automated system that monitors and manages the environmental
variables of an indoor vertical farming setup in real-time. The platform should collect
sensor data, automate routine agricultural tasks, and provide actionable, data-driven
insights to help users maximize crop yield while strictly minimizing water and electricity
usage. It should be scalable enough for a small community garden or an indoor campus
greenhouse.
_UTMxHackathon’_

**4. What the solution should solve**
Participants can build a working hardware prototype (optional) paired with a software
dashboard. Key features could include:
● Real-time monitoring using sensors for temperature, ambient humidity, soil
moisture, and water pH levels.
● Automated control systems to adjust LED lighting spectrums, activate cooling
fans, or trigger hydroponic water pumps based on specific plant profiles.
● A centralized web or mobile dashboard that visualizes growth metrics, resource
consumption, and historical data trends.
● Predictive alerts that notify users of system anomalies, such as a water reservoir
running dry or temperatures exceeding safe thresholds.
● An AI-driven recommendation engine that suggests optimal harvest times or
adjustments to nutrient mixtures based on sensor inputs.

**The solution should be:**
● A functional hardware (optional) and software prototype demonstrating real-world
data collection.
● Interactive and user-friendly, allowing individuals without deep agricultural
expertise to manage a system.
● Scalable to adapt to different types of crops or larger vertical farming racks
**Note:** The features and tools listed above are provided as
guidance only. Participants are strongly encouraged to explore
alternative approaches that meaningfully address the problem
statement.
